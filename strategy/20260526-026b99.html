How today's AI developments apply to your business.

Executive Summary

I'm watching two critical shifts that business leaders can't ignore today. First, Google is preparing to fundamentally remake search with agentic AI in 2026, which means the way your customers find your business is about to change dramatically. When Google transforms how search works, it affects every marketing strategy, every SEO investment, every customer acquisition plan. Second, even the biggest tech companies are admitting they're learning AI security in real time. Google, the company reshaping search, doesn't have AI security figured out yet. Neither does anyone else. This matters because businesses are deploying AI tools without understanding the security implications, and there's no playbook to follow. The companies building these systems are writing the rules as they go. If you're a VP of Marketing or Creative Director being asked to show ROI on AI investments while navigating security concerns your IT department can't answer, you're not behind. You're dealing with the same uncertainty as trillion-dollar tech companies. The difference is you need to act anyway.

Strategic Shifts · May 26

SHIFT 1 PREPARE This year AI agent interface consolidation

Search is becoming an AI agent battlefield with customer acquisition implications

Google's planned 2026 search overhaul using agentic AI represents more than a feature update. It's a fundamental change from providing information to taking actions. When search engines become AI agents that can complete tasks rather than just surface links, the entire customer journey changes. This shift is happening while SpaceX bets its future on AI to compete with tech giants, and Amazon releases wearables that blend AI interaction with daily activities. The pattern shows major companies racing to control the AI interface layer where customers will interact with brands. Historical context validates this trend: over the past week, we've seen Google rebuild search with AI-powered interfaces, Meta restructure 8,000 employees toward AI focus, and enterprise AI spending surge 47% despite ROI struggles. The search remake isn't isolated; it's part of a broader battle for AI-powered customer touchpoints.

Who This Affects

Marketing Teams

SEO strategies, content marketing, and customer acquisition funnels become obsolete when search stops being about ranking and starts being about AI agent recommendations.

Leadership/C-Suite

Customer acquisition costs and digital marketing budgets need fundamental recalculation as the primary discovery mechanism shifts from search optimization to AI agent relationships.

What To Do This Week

  • Audit your current SEO and content strategy investments before Q4 budget planning to understand what becomes irrelevant in an agent-driven search world
  • Identify which customer touchpoints could be replaced by AI agents and develop direct relationship strategies that bypass traditional search discovery
  • Test how your brand and products perform in current AI agent interactions (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) to understand what agents know about you

SHIFT 2 ACT NOW This month AI security standardization

AI security becomes a competitive advantage as everyone learns in real time

The admission that even Google is learning AI security measures as they go reveals a critical market reality: no one has solved AI security yet. This creates both risk and opportunity. While companies like Google, OpenAI, and Nvidia collaborate on watermarking technology like SynthID, and governments scramble to address AI voice cloning of deceased pilots, the security playbook is being written in real time. Companies that develop strong AI security practices now gain competitive advantage over those that deploy blindly. This connects to last week's pattern of workers secretly resisting AI adoption while exaggerating their skills, and the continued concerns about AI data training without permission. The security uncertainty isn't slowing adoption; AI spending is surging 47% despite these unknowns. But businesses that audit their security exposure before adopting new tools will outperform those that assume the vendors have it figured out.

Who This Affects

Operations

IT departments must create AI security protocols without industry standards, making every deployment a custom security assessment.

Leadership/C-Suite

AI governance decisions can't be delegated to vendors or delayed until regulations arrive; executive teams must set internal standards ahead of industry clarity.

What To Do This Week

  • Audit every AI tool currently in use to understand what data is being shared and whether training opt-outs are enabled
  • Establish internal AI security protocols now rather than waiting for vendor guidance or industry standards
  • Document AI usage policies that address data sharing, training consent, and content watermarking before Q3 tool renewals

SHIFT 3 PREPARE This quarter Workforce displacement acceleration

AI automation accelerates job displacement faster than workforce transition plans

ClickUp's mass layoffs signal that AI-driven workforce changes are happening now, not in some distant future. This follows last week's pattern of NYC Comptroller warning about 110,000 potential job losses, Federal Reserve concerns about displacement before job creation, and Meta's 8,000 AI-focused layoffs. Meanwhile, Figure AI's viral package-handling robots demonstrate that physical automation is advancing alongside digital AI. The displacement-to-rehiring gap I've been concerned about is materializing faster than expected. Companies are achieving AI productivity gains and immediately reducing headcount, but they're not simultaneously creating new roles that displaced workers can transition into. Ferrari's partnership with IBM for AI fan engagement and the success of AI science assistants in drug research show new opportunities emerging, but the skills gap between eliminated roles and new positions is widening.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Workforce planning must account for both productivity gains and social responsibility for displaced employees, requiring transition support strategies.

Operations

HR departments need retraining programs and role transition plans before automation deployments, not after layoff announcements.

What To Do This Week

  • Identify which roles in your organization could be automated in the next 18 months and create transition plans before implementing AI systems
  • Calculate the true cost of AI deployment including employee transition support, not just tool subscription fees and productivity gains
  • Pilot AI augmentation programs that enhance employee capabilities rather than replace them entirely
