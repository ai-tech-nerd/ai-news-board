How today's AI developments apply to your business.

Executive Summary

Today marks a fundamental shift in AI economics: the money is flowing toward solutions that cut costs rather than expand capabilities. I see three critical patterns converging. First, the economics are forcing consolidation: OpenAI and Anthropic are both racing toward massive IPOs (targeting $1T+ valuations) while simultaneously cutting job displacement warnings to appease investors. Second, enterprises are hitting AI budget walls faster than expected, creating a market for platforms like Glean that promise cost reduction over expansion. Third, the infrastructure itself is being rebuilt for machines, not humans, which means businesses need to audit their current systems before they become obsolete. What concerns me most is that while CEOs are getting $370K AI bonuses at Samsung and VCs are throwing $65B at Anthropic, the fundamental question remains unanswered: do you know what AI tools you're currently using and why? The audit-before-adopt principle has never been more critical, because the money suggests this train isn't slowing down.

Strategic Shifts · May 29

SHIFT 1 ACT NOW This month cost compression

AI economics pivot from growth to cost optimization as enterprise budgets hit walls

The AI market is experiencing a fundamental economic reorientation. Glean crossed $300M revenue specifically by positioning itself as a cost-cutting solution for AI budgets, not a capability enhancer. This comes as companies like Uber burned through their entire 2026 AI budget by April. Meanwhile, OpenAI's Sam Altman is walking back job displacement warnings ahead of their $1T IPO, and Samsung workers are receiving $370K bonuses tied to 750% profit increases from AI memory demand. The shift represents a maturation from 'AI can do anything' to 'AI must pay for itself.' Enterprises are discovering that unlimited AI experimentation creates budget crises, forcing a pivot toward tools that demonstrate clear ROI through cost reduction rather than capability expansion.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Must shift AI strategy from growth investment to cost optimization, with clear ROI metrics becoming mandatory for any AI spending.

Operations

Need to audit current AI spending and identify which tools are providing measurable efficiency gains versus those burning budget without clear returns.

What To Do This Week

  • Conduct immediate audit of all AI tool subscriptions and usage across departments to identify budget drains
  • Implement ROI tracking for existing AI tools before adding new capabilities
  • Evaluate cost-optimization platforms like Glean that can consolidate multiple AI functions

SHIFT 2 PREPARE This quarter infrastructure transformation

Infrastructure rebuild prioritizes machine consumption over human experience

A fundamental architectural shift is occurring where internet infrastructure and content are being optimized for AI consumption rather than human users. Apple is cramming Google's multi-trillion parameter Gemini model into iPhones to power Siri, YouTube is implementing automatic AI video labeling, and the broader internet is being restructured around machine-readable formats. This isn't just about AI tools; it's about the underlying infrastructure being redesigned for AI-first consumption. The implication is that businesses operating with human-centric digital strategies will find themselves increasingly disadvantaged as the infrastructure they depend on becomes optimized for machine interaction.

Who This Affects

Marketing Teams

Must redesign content strategy and website architecture to be discoverable and consumable by AI systems, not just search engines.

Engineering

Need to rebuild APIs and data structures to support machine consumption while maintaining human usability.

What To Do This Week

  • Audit current website and content architecture for AI discoverability and machine readability
  • Begin restructuring content and data for AI consumption while maintaining human accessibility
  • Evaluate whether your digital presence will be findable and usable by AI systems

SHIFT 3 ACT NOW This month security vulnerabilities

Security vulnerabilities in AI agents create enterprise liability gaps

Critical security vulnerabilities are affecting millions of deployed AI agents while malicious actors are actively targeting AI systems. A developer inserted prompt injection attacks into code specifically targeting AI-assisted programming, and a critical vulnerability in an open source package is compromising millions of AI agents. Meanwhile, research shows LLMs continue believing false information even after explicit warnings about inaccuracy. This creates a dangerous combination: AI systems that can be compromised, won't properly weight contradictory information, and are being deployed at scale without adequate security oversight.

Who This Affects

Engineering

Must implement security protocols for AI systems that go beyond traditional cybersecurity to include prompt injection and model manipulation threats.

Leadership/C-Suite

Face potential liability for AI system breaches that could compromise sensitive data or make incorrect decisions based on manipulated inputs.

What To Do This Week

  • Audit all deployed AI agents and systems for security vulnerabilities and update to patched versions
  • Implement human oversight protocols for AI decisions, especially those involving sensitive data or critical business functions
  • Establish AI security policies that address prompt injection and model manipulation threats

SHIFT 4 MONITOR This quarter consolidation

Massive funding rounds signal AI industry consolidation around proven revenue models

The AI funding landscape is consolidating around companies with proven revenue streams rather than experimental capabilities. Anthropic raised $65B approaching $1T valuation, OpenAI is targeting $1T for their Q4 IPO, and NVIDIA committed $150B to Taiwan operations. Meanwhile, specialized companies like Trajectory raised $15M with a team from major AI labs, and a chip startup secured $135M betting memory beats compute. The pattern shows investors backing established players with clear revenue models while also funding specialized solutions that solve specific technical problems. This suggests the era of general-purpose AI experimentation is ending in favor of targeted applications.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Must choose between established AI platforms from consolidated players versus specialized tools that solve specific business problems.

Operations

Need to evaluate whether current AI tool mix provides specialized value or if consolidation to major platforms would improve efficiency.

What To Do This Week

  • Track which AI vendors in your current tool stack have sustainable funding and revenue models
  • Evaluate whether specialized AI tools provide enough value to justify separate contracts versus consolidating to major platforms
  • Prepare contingency plans for smaller AI vendors that may be acquired or discontinued
