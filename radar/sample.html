Scientists Capture New Ocean Floor Formation in Real Time

In 2024, a swarm of earthquakes in the Indian Ocean caused tectonic plates to abruptly separate, adding more than three feet of new seafloor in a single event. Scientists had instruments deployed just two months before the earthquakes struck, allowing them to observe the process for the first time ever. The findings, published this week, offer the most detailed look yet at how Earth recycles its own crust.

More than three feet of new seafloor was added in a single tectonic event in the Indian Ocean in 2024.
