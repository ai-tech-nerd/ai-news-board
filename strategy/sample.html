How today's AI developments apply to your business.

Executive Summary

I've watched three distinct AI waves hit different parts of the economy this week, and today's data shows we're entering the steepest part of the displacement curve. Government officials at the highest levels are now warning what I've been tracking for months: jobs are disappearing faster than new ones are being created. NYC's comptroller warns 110,000 jobs could vanish, the Federal Reserve confirms displacement will come before creation, and meanwhile companies are using AI as justification for cuts that may be backfiring. But here's the disconnect that should alarm every business leader: while 63% of workers exaggerate their AI skills to seem current, 81% actively discourage AI adoption at work. This creates a dangerous double bind where companies think their workforce is AI-ready when they're actually resistant, setting up both sides for failure. The businesses that audit their current capabilities and address this skills-resistance gap now will have a massive advantage over those operating on false assumptions about their team's readiness.

Strategic Shifts · May 25

SHIFT 1 ACT NOW This quarter workforce displacement

The job displacement timeline has accelerated beyond policy preparation

Multiple government officials issued unprecedented warnings this week about AI job displacement happening faster than job creation. NYC Comptroller Mark Levine warned 110,000 jobs could be eliminated in New York alone, while a Federal Reserve Governor cautioned displacement will come before creation. This isn't theoretical anymore: PwC laid off 600 executive assistants in February, Meta justified 8,000 cuts as AI-driven restructuring, and AI was the leading reason for job cuts in April 2026. The pattern shows displacement concentrated in administrative, repetitive, and coordination roles first. What makes this different from historical technological displacement is the speed and the government's acknowledgment they're unprepared. When the Federal Reserve is warning about timing, that signals the transition period could be more disruptive than previous technology shifts.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Need immediate workforce planning for roles most vulnerable to AI displacement, particularly administrative and coordination positions.

Operations

Must identify which roles are truly at risk versus which can be augmented with AI tools to increase productivity.

Marketing Teams

Executive assistant roles supporting marketing operations are among the first being eliminated, requiring process restructuring.

What To Do This Week

  • Audit your organization's administrative and repetitive roles to identify which are most vulnerable to AI replacement
  • Create transition plans for affected employees, including retraining programs for augmented roles rather than eliminated ones
  • Develop internal AI literacy programs to bridge the skills gap before external pressure forces hasty decisions

SHIFT 2 ACT NOW This month enterprise adoption

Worker AI competency is a facade hiding active resistance

A GCheck study revealed the most dangerous misconception in AI adoption: 63% of workers exaggerate their AI skills to appear current, while 81% secretly discourage AI use at work. Gen Z leads this pattern at 80% exaggerating skills. This creates a 'double distortion' where leadership believes their workforce is AI-ready based on inflated self-reporting, while employees actively undermine adoption efforts. Companies are making strategic AI investments assuming capability that doesn't exist and facing internal resistance they don't know about. The gap between perceived readiness and actual resistance explains why many AI initiatives fail despite significant investment. This pattern connects to the broader displacement anxiety: workers sense the threat but lack the skills to adapt, so they resist rather than upskill.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Operating under false assumptions about workforce AI readiness, leading to failed initiatives and wasted investment.

Operations

AI tool rollouts meeting unexpected resistance from employees who claimed readiness but actively discourage adoption.

Marketing Teams

Team members may be sabotaging AI creative tools while reporting proficiency, reducing campaign efficiency.

What To Do This Week

  • Conduct anonymous assessments of actual AI tool usage and comfort levels, not self-reported skills
  • Implement pilot programs with voluntary participation to identify genuine AI advocates within your organization
  • Create psychological safety for employees to admit AI knowledge gaps without career consequences

SHIFT 3 PREPARE This year AGI development

AI capabilities are leaping beyond human mathematical reasoning

Google DeepMind's AlphaProof Nexus autonomously solved nine open mathematical problems that stumped humans for decades, including two unsolved for 56 years, at a cost of just hundreds of dollars per solution. Meanwhile, OpenAI's internal reasoning model disproved an 80-year-old mathematical belief tied to Erdős' 1946 unit distance problem. These aren't incremental improvements in existing capabilities; they represent AI systems making original mathematical discoveries that advance human knowledge. The cost efficiency is staggering: problems that required decades of human mathematical expertise are being solved for the price of a laptop. This suggests we're approaching a threshold where AI doesn't just process information faster than humans, but genuinely reasons through complex problems in ways that produce new knowledge.

Who This Affects

Engineering

AI systems may soon outpace human engineers in complex problem-solving, requiring shift from execution to oversight and validation.

Operations

Cost structure for complex analysis and research could drop dramatically, enabling previously impossible projects.

Leadership/C-Suite

Competitive advantages based on human expertise may erode rapidly as AI democratizes advanced problem-solving capabilities.

What To Do This Week

  • Identify your organization's most complex analytical challenges that could benefit from AI reasoning systems
  • Evaluate which competitive advantages depend on human expertise that AI might replicate at much lower cost
  • Begin testing AI systems on simplified versions of your hardest problems to understand current capabilities

SHIFT 4 MONITOR This year consolidation

Industry consolidation around AI infrastructure standards is accelerating

Google's SynthID watermarking technology is being adopted by major competitors including OpenAI and Nvidia, signaling unusual collaboration on AI content identification standards. This follows the pattern from earlier this week where Andrej Karpathy joined Anthropic and Google positioned itself as a design tools competitor at I/O 2026. The industry is simultaneously competing fiercely on capabilities while collaborating on infrastructure and safety standards. Watermarking adoption across competitors suggests regulatory pressure or market demand for AI content identification is forcing cooperation. Companies are recognizing that some AI challenges, particularly around security and identification, require industry-wide solutions rather than competitive advantages.

Who This Affects

Marketing Teams

Need to understand which AI-generated content will be watermarked and how this affects campaign authenticity and disclosure requirements.

Creative Teams

AI-generated creative work may be automatically identified, requiring new workflows for content verification and disclosure.

Leadership/C-Suite

Industry standard adoption may reduce competitive differentiation while increasing compliance requirements.

What To Do This Week

  • Track which AI content creation tools in your stack support watermarking standards
  • Develop policies for disclosing AI-generated content before watermarking makes it automatically detectable
  • Monitor industry collaboration patterns to predict which AI capabilities will become commoditized

SHIFT 5 PREPARE 12-18 months enterprise adoption

Physical AI systems are reaching commercial viability

Figure AI's humanoid robots handling packages went viral this week, demonstrating advanced manipulation capabilities in warehouse operations. This coincides with Amazon launching the Bee wearable device and Google's continued push into AI glasses partnerships. Physical AI is moving beyond prototypes into real-world applications that people can see and interact with. The viral nature of the Figure AI videos suggests public readiness for AI systems in physical spaces, while the mixed reviews of Amazon's Bee wearable show the privacy and acceptance challenges these devices face. Unlike software AI that works behind the scenes, physical AI systems require public acceptance and regulatory approval, creating different adoption dynamics.

Who This Affects

Operations

Warehouse, logistics, and repetitive physical tasks could be automated sooner than expected, requiring workforce planning.

Marketing Teams

Consumer acceptance of AI devices affects marketing strategies for physical products and services.

Leadership/C-Suite

Investment in physical automation may deliver ROI faster than anticipated if public acceptance accelerates.

What To Do This Week

  • Evaluate which physical processes in your operations could benefit from AI automation within 12-18 months
  • Research public perception of AI devices relevant to your customer base to inform product development
  • Calculate ROI timelines for physical AI investments based on accelerating capability development
