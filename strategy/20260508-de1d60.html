How today's AI developments apply to your business.

Executive Summary

I'm seeing three forces colliding that every business leader needs to understand right now. First, AI companies are running out of compute and scrambling for solutions: Anthropic just leased SpaceX's entire supercluster despite Musk calling them 'Misanthropic' six months ago. That's desperation math. Second, these same companies are pushing hard into finance and enterprise tools, all launching simultaneously this week. When competitors coordinate timing like that, they're chasing the same urgent market signal. Third, crypto companies are using AI as justification for mass layoffs while OpenAI's own executives testify that safety reviews were skipped. The message is clear: AI capabilities are accelerating faster than oversight, faster than infrastructure, and faster than most businesses can adapt. The window for strategic planning is closing. Companies that haven't audited their current tools and processes are about to be blindsided by both opportunity and displacement.

Strategic Shifts · May 08

SHIFT 1 PREPARE This quarter infrastructure constraints

Compute scarcity is driving unexpected partnerships and reshaping AI competitive dynamics

Anthropic signing a deal to lease SpaceX's entire Colossus supercluster represents more than just a business transaction. This is Anthropic, backed by Amazon and Google, turning to Elon Musk's operation despite his public criticism of them as 'Misanthropic.' The 220K+ NVIDIA GPU cluster in Memphis gives them massive computational power, but it also reveals how desperate the situation has become. When you combine this with TSMC pivoting to wind power specifically to meet AI chip manufacturing demands, we're seeing infrastructure constraints force companies into partnerships they wouldn't have considered six months ago. This isn't just about Anthropic needing more GPUs. It signals that compute availability, not just capability, is becoming the determining factor in AI competition. Companies with access to compute will have strategic advantages that pure software innovation can't overcome.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Budget planning for AI initiatives must now factor in compute scarcity and potentially higher costs as demand outstrips supply.

Engineering

Architecture decisions around cloud vs. edge computing become more critical as enterprise compute resources become constrained.

Operations

Vendor relationships and infrastructure partnerships may need evaluation as compute becomes a strategic differentiator.

What To Do This Week

  • Audit your current cloud computing contracts and usage patterns before renewal cycles hit peak demand periods
  • Evaluate whether your AI initiatives require high-compute resources and identify alternative providers beyond the big three
  • Consider hybrid on-premise solutions for compute-intensive tasks to reduce dependency on cloud availability

SHIFT 2 ACT NOW This month enterprise adoption

Finance departments are becoming the primary AI battleground for enterprise adoption

Anthropic, OpenAI, and Perplexity all launched finance-specific AI tools simultaneously this week. That's not coincidence, that's market intelligence. Finance departments represent the perfect storm for AI adoption: high-value repetitive work, clear ROI measurement capabilities, and direct access to budget decisions. Claude launched financial service templates, OpenAI partnered with PwC for finance functions, and Perplexity extended its computer agent to financial services with licensed data from Morningstar and Pitchbook. This coordinated push suggests these companies have identified finance as the department most likely to demonstrate clear value and drive organization-wide adoption. Finance teams that prove AI ROI become internal champions for broader deployment. Finance teams that don't risk being seen as obstacles to innovation.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Finance department AI adoption will likely become the pilot that determines organization-wide AI strategy and budget allocation.

Operations

Financial reporting, budgeting, and analysis workflows will require integration with AI tools to maintain competitive analysis capabilities.

What To Do This Week

  • Inventory your finance team's current software stack and identify which tasks consume the most time weekly
  • Run an AI Opportunity Assessment specifically for financial workflows before competitors gain advantages in analysis speed
  • Establish clear ROI metrics for any finance AI pilot to create internal case studies for broader adoption

SHIFT 3 ACT NOW This month governance breakdown

Safety oversight is breaking down as AI capabilities advance faster than governance structures

OpenAI's former CTO Mira Murati testified that Sam Altman lied about safety reviews and undermined her authority, specifically claiming legal clearance for models that hadn't completed safety review. This comes as Anthropic's Mythos found 271 vulnerabilities with almost no false positives in Mozilla's systems, demonstrating AI's growing capability for both security testing and potential security breaches. The pattern emerging from both the lawsuit testimony and the increasing pressure from Musk's legal action is that internal safety processes are struggling to keep pace with development speed. When former executives testify about skipped safety protocols while AI systems demonstrate unprecedented security analysis capabilities, we're seeing the human oversight gap I've been warning about. The technology is advancing faster than our ability to understand its implications, and even the companies building it can't maintain consistent internal controls.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Due diligence on AI vendor security practices and safety protocols becomes critical for risk management and potential liability.

Engineering

Internal AI deployment must include robust testing and validation processes since vendor safety claims may not reflect reality.

What To Do This Week

  • Implement human-in-the-loop validation for all AI outputs affecting business decisions, regardless of vendor safety claims
  • Audit your data security practices around AI tools to ensure no proprietary information is being used for training
  • Establish clear escalation procedures for AI system errors or unexpected outputs before problems compound

SHIFT 4 PREPARE This quarter workforce displacement

AI-driven workforce displacement is accelerating beyond tech companies

The pattern is expanding beyond traditional tech layoffs. Coinbase cut 14% of staff while CEO Brian Armstrong emphasized rebuilding as an 'intelligence' company leveraging AI across all operations. Block, PayPal, and Crypto.com followed with similar announcements, all citing AI advancement as justification. This represents the displacement-to-rehiring gap I've been tracking, but it's happening faster than expected. Coinbase specifically mentioned rebuilding as an intelligence company, which means they're not just cutting costs, they're fundamentally changing what skills they value. When financial services companies start positioning themselves as AI companies first, we're seeing the beginning of industry-wide restructuring that will spread beyond crypto and fintech.

Who This Affects

Leadership/C-Suite

Workforce planning must account for both displacement and the need to hire AI-skilled specialists to remain competitive.

Operations

Job descriptions and skill requirements need immediate review to identify roles at risk and skills gaps for AI oversight.

What To Do This Week

  • Conduct skills inventory to identify which current employees have AI aptitude and which roles could be augmented vs. replaced
  • Plan retraining programs for employees whose roles will change rather than disappear entirely
  • Budget for AI specialists and subject matter experts who can oversee automated systems effectively
